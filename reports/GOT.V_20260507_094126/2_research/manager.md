**Recommendation**: Overweight

**Rationale**: Both analysts agree that Goliath Resources (GOT.V) is a pre‑revenue, cash‑burning junior explorer, but they diverge sharply on the significance of its current balance sheet and catalyst outlook. The bear side correctly flags the massive 253% share increase, the $45.3M trailing 12‑month cash burn and the fact that the $49.4M cash runway only covers roughly one year of current‑rate exploration spending. Those points highlight a real risk of another dilutive raise if drill results don’t meet expectations. The bull side counters that the $26.3M capital raise was completed at deeply discounted levels, leaving the company with $49.4M in liquid assets, zero long‑term debt, and a current ratio of 4.34x – a rare position for a junior explorer. The bull narrative also points to a classic bottoming technical pattern (RSI bullish divergence, MACD histogram turning positive, ATR compression and a double‑bottom around $1.47‑$1.55) and an institutional vote of confidence via Rob McEwen’s warrant extension. Finally, the Stifel C$5.00 price target implies a >200% upside, and macro tailwinds for gold add a supportive backdrop. Weighing the arguments, the funding profile, the technical reversal signals and the institutional endorsement together outweigh the dilution and cash‑burn concerns, especially given that the company now has more than a year of runway to deliver a catalyst without further equity issuance. Therefore the dominant view is bullish, but the inherent execution risk of junior mining means we stay disciplined on sizing.

Recommendation: Overweight
